Quick Hits

From auditing the site today, there are a variety of tactical changes that could be made immediately.

01

Traffic is declining

Per SimilarWeb, tanium.com gets ~267K visits/month — but traffic dropped 12.4%. This is likely due to the rise of GEO, with more research being done inside chat agents. Tanium needs to solidify its GEO/AIO strategy to account for the loss of traditional organic traffic.

02

Personalization is missing in action

A CISO at a Fortune 100 bank and an IT admin at a mid-market retailer currently see the exact same homepage. No persona-based content paths, no industry-specific landing pages, no ABM-personalized experiences. 6Sense is already in the stack — it could be used immediately.

03

Rotating hero carousels are conversion killers

The homepage hero includes a 3-slide auto-rotating carousel. This is one of the most well-documented conversion problems in B2B web design — the majority of visitors never see slides 2 or 3. The Gartner MQ win and brand narrative are largely invisible to most traffic.

04

The demo form is heavy for cold traffic

The "See a Demo" form requires 7 fields on first touch — company email, first/last name, job title, phone, company name, and country — with no lighter conversion path or progressive profiling option. This creates unnecessary friction at the most critical conversion moment.

05

Events calendar needs more owned events

Currently seeing 7 upcoming events listed — almost all third-party trade shows. The calendar needs more webinars and demo opportunities that prospective clients can try. Fewer form fields on these would help too.

06

Interactive conversion tools are absent

There aren't many places for prospects to explore on their own terms. ROI calculators, interactive product tours, and self-guided demos would help. Competitors like CrowdStrike are moving toward these to warm up leads earlier in the journey.

07

No visible nurture architecture

The site doesn't give visitors many next steps. When finishing an article or page, there should be "if you read this, here's what's next" content flows — tracked and used to fuel progressive profiling and further personalization.

08

JavaScript dependency breaks the site

The site fails entirely when JavaScript is disabled — a real problem for bot crawlers and accessibility. Delivering pages as static assets would fix this and meaningfully speed up the site for everyone.

A Test from My Time at NetApp

Asking Questions

One small but important test I ran with my team was to simply ask questions to our customers on the site. It started as a small test on one page, but grew into whole-site profiling efforts. The assumption: customers in the B2B space don't want to waste time, and will tell you what they're looking for if you make it easy. So we just asked: what do you want?

Tanium could benefit from a similar strategy — profiling the customer throughout the process and offering relevant information at every stage. Simply ask: what industry are you in? What are you looking to secure? What issues have you had in the past? It makes the site feel more human, and speeds up the entire sales process.
